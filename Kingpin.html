Creation

 Wilson Fisk, or the Kingpin first appeared in The Amazing Spider-Man #50 in 1967 and was created Stan Lee and John Romita Sr. Kingpin's name is a play off of the Mob, as in comics he is the mob boss of all mob bosses. While he originally appeared as a villain of Spider-Man, he is actually the archnemesis of Daredevil, and also fights the punisher. While appeared as a big man, it isn't fat but rather pure muscle, which he uses in hand to hand combat against his enemies.

Origins

 Originally a poor child in New York City who was bullied for his obesity, Wilson Fisk trained until it was all muscle, and began working under a mob boss Don Rigoletto. After working his way up to Rigoletto's right hand man, he turned and killed his mentor and took control of his enterprise. He continued to expand until he ruled almost all of NYC's underworld, earning the title of Kingpin.
